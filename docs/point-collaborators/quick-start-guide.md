# Quick Start Guide for Syskit Point

In this article, you'll find an overview of everything you need know to start using **Syskit Point as a workspace owner, team owner, or manager**. 


## What is Syskit Point?

**Syskit Point** is a **management and governance platform for Microsoft 365** that helps you keep your sites, teams, groups, and shared content **secure, organized, and up to date**.

As a workspace owner, Syskit Point lets you:

* See what you are responsible for in Microsoft 365 at a glance
* Complete short review tasks that keep your workspaces healthy
* Manage who has access to your sites, teams, and groups
* Report on activity and sharing across your workspaces

You don't need to be a Microsoft 365 expert to use Syskit Point. Most of your work happens through guided tasks and clear screens with simple instructions.

## How to Access Syskit Point

Your admin will share a link to Syskit Point with you, or you can open it directly from any email notification you receive from it.

To sign in:

* Open the **Syskit Point URL** provided by your admin
* Sign in with your **work Microsoft 365 account**
* The **Home** screen opens


## The Syskit Point Home Screen

Once you sign in, the **Home** screen gives you quick access to everything that's relevant to you as an owner:

* **Sites** - all SharePoint sites you own
* **Microsoft Teams & Groups** - all teams and groups you own
* **Users** - users you manage
* **Dashboard** - a visual summary of your workspaces, users, external sharing, and storage
* **Reports** - pre-built reports you can generate and export
* **My Tasks** - tasks waiting for you to review or resolve
* **Scheduled Reports** - reports that arrive in your inbox automatically
* **Alerts** - notifications about important changes in your workspaces

You only see the data for workspaces and users you're responsible for. Nothing else is visible to you.

## What You'll Be Asked to Do

Most of your day-to-day work in Syskit Point happens in four main areas.

### 1. Resolve Your Tasks

Your organization sets governance rules, and Syskit Point turns those rules into short, easy tasks. You'll see them in **My Tasks** on the Home screen. Your most common tasks will include the following policies:

| Task Type | What You Need to Do |
| --- | --- |
| **Workspace Review** | Review who has access to your workspaces. Keep, change, or remove permissions for internal and external users. |
| **Inactive Workspaces** | A workspace you own hasn't been used. Decide whether to keep, archive, or delete it. |
| **Minimum Owners** | Your workspace has too few owners. Promote an existing member or add a new owner to meet policy. |
| **Maximum Owners** | Your workspace has too many owners. Remove or demote owners to meet policy. |
| **Orphaned Workspace** | A workspace has no active owner. Assign a new owner or choose to archive or delete it. |
| **Metadata / Privacy Review** | Confirm or update the workspace description, sensitivity label, or privacy setting. |

Each task walks you through the steps, so you always know what's expected of you.

### 2. Complete a Workspace Review

A **Workspace Review** is a scheduled check that your admin sets up to make sure every workspace follows your organization's policies. You'll receive an email when a review is due. The email contains a **Review Workspace** button that opens a guided wizard inside Syskit Point.

In a workspace review, you may be asked to:

* Confirm or update the **owners and members**
* Review **guest access and external sharing**
* Check **files and folders** that are shared widely
* Resolve any **policy issues** flagged by Syskit Point
* Decide whether the workspace should be **kept or removed**

The wizard guides you step by step, and you can save your progress and finish later.

### 3. Manage Access to Your Workspaces

You can manage who has access to your sites, teams, and groups directly from Syskit Point — no need to switch between different Microsoft 365 admin centers.

From the **Sites**, **Teams**, or **Groups** screen, you can:

* **Add or remove owners and members**
* **Manage external sharing** and guest users
* **Request a new workspace** if you need one
* **Request access** to a workspace you don't yet own

Changes you make in Syskit Point are applied to Microsoft 365 automatically.

### 4. Generate Reports

The **Reports** section gives you ready-made reports for the workspaces you own — for example, who has access, what's being shared externally, or how storage is being used.

You can:

* **Export** any report to Excel or PDF
* **Schedule** a report to be emailed to you on a regular basis
* **Filter and group** the data to focus on what matters

## Emails You May Receive

Syskit Point only emails you when there's something for you to do. The most common emails are:

* **Task assigned** — you have a new task in My Tasks
* **Workspace Review request** — it's time to review one or more of your workspaces
* **Reminder** — a task or review is approaching its due date
* **Scheduled report** — a report you scheduled is ready

Every email contains a button that takes you directly to the right screen in Syskit Point.

## Tips for Getting Started

* **Resolve tasks on time.** Tasks have due dates. Completing them keeps your workspaces compliant and helps protect your organization from security risks.
* **Use the dashboard.** It's the fastest way to spot workspaces with too many guests, unusual sharing, or no recent activity.
* **Ask before deleting.** If you're unsure whether a workspace is still needed, start a chat with co-owners directly from the review screen.
* **Trust the guided steps.** Every workflow in Syskit Point is designed so you don't need to remember technical details — just follow the wizard.
